# Local Business Outreach Scripts
## Email and Phone Templates by Website Situation

This file is a practical sales playbook for using leads exported from `Local Business Lead Finder`.

Use it with these workbook sheets first:

1. **Send Now** – best candidates. Prioritize these.
2. **No Website Offer** – businesses with no real website.
3. **Platform Website Offer** – businesses using directories, social pages, booking platforms, or marketplace pages instead of their own site.
4. **Manual Review** – check manually before contacting.
5. **Visual Review** – websites that load but look visually weak; review screenshots/site first.
6. **Looks Fine** – lower urgency; contact only if you have time or a strong niche angle.

Do not treat the scripts as copy-paste spam. Personalize at least one sentence for every business.

---

# 1. Universal Outreach Rules

## Before contacting a business

Check these fields in the Excel file:

- `business_name`
- `city`
- `sector`
- `website_url`
- `website_type`
- `outreach_decision`
- `priority`
- `final_opportunity_score`
- `website_pain_score`
- `visual_pain_score`
- `data_quality_status`
- `website_audit.load_confidence` if visible in the sheet
- email / phone fields if available

Only contact leads from clean action sheets:
- Send Now
- No Website Offer
- Platform Website Offer
- Manual Review after checking
- Visual Review after checking

Avoid or postpone:
- Data Quality Review
- Hard Skip
- Looks Fine unless you have a good reason

## Tone

Use a simple founder-to-owner tone:

- Direct
- Helpful
- Not corporate
- No fake urgency
- No exaggerated promises
- No long agency-style pitch

The goal is not to “sell a website” immediately.
The goal is to start a conversation.

## Main offer framing

You are offering:

- A modern website
- Better first impression
- Clear service pages
- Better mobile experience
- Contact buttons / WhatsApp / phone calls
- Google-friendly local structure
- Optional before/after website mockup
- Fast delivery for local service businesses

Avoid saying:

- “Your website is bad”
- “Your current website is terrible”
- “You are losing customers”
- “I guarantee more leads”
- “I found errors in your business”

Use softer wording:

- “I noticed your online presence could be improved”
- “Your business looks active, but the website does not fully show that”
- “I think a cleaner website could make you look more professional”
- “I made a quick note because you seem like a good fit”

# 2. Sheet-by-Sheet Contact Strategy

## Send Now

Best leads. Contact first.

Common reasons:
- Website has visible issues
- Contact path exists
- Business fit is good
- Score is strong

Best approach:
- Email first if email exists
- Call if no email or if local service business looks very active
- Mention one specific issue

## No Website Offer

These businesses have no real website.

Best approach:
- Offer a simple starter website
- Do not criticize them
- Mention that many customers check Google before calling

## Platform Website Offer

These businesses rely on platforms/directories/social pages.

Examples:
- Facebook page
- Instagram page
- nlcompanies.org
- telefoonboek.nl
- openingstijden.nl
- cylex.nl
- werkspot.nl
- homedeal.nl
- other directory/marketplace pages

Best approach:
- Explain that a business-owned website is more professional and controllable
- Do not insult the platform
- Position the website as their “own home online”

## Manual Review

Check manually before contacting.

Use when:
- Website audit is blocked or uncertain
- Website loads strangely
- Contact details need confirmation
- You are not sure if the business is real / active

Best approach:
- Open the website
- Check Google Business Profile
- Decide whether to move it mentally into Send Now, Platform Offer, No Website, or Skip

## Visual Review

Website loads, but visual audit found visible design pain.

Best approach:
- Open website manually
- Look for outdated design, poor mobile layout, weak first impression, bad hero section, low-quality images, confusing navigation
- Contact with a visual-first message

# 4. Phone Scripts

## 4.1 General Cold Call Script

You:

Hi, is this {{business_name}}?

Business:

Yes.

You:

Great. My name is {{your_name}}. I’ll keep it short. I found your business while looking at {{sector}} companies in {{city}}. I help local service businesses improve their website or online presence so customers can understand the services faster and contact them more easily.

I noticed there may be some room to improve your website / online presence. Is the website something you handle yourself, or does someone else manage it?

Then listen.

If they are interested:

I can make a quick suggestion or simple example for free, just to show what I mean. What is the best email to send it to?

If they are busy:

No problem. I can send a short email instead. What is the best email address?

If they say no:

No worries at all. Thanks for your time, have a good day.

# 6. Objection Handling

## “We already have a website.”

Reply:

That makes sense. I’m not saying you do not have one. My point is more about whether the website gives the strongest possible first impression and makes it easy for customers to contact you.

I can send one or two quick suggestions. If they are not useful, no problem.

## “We are not interested.”

Reply:

No problem at all. Thanks for your time.

## “How much does it cost?”

Reply:

It depends on the size, but for local service businesses I usually keep it simple.

A basic website can include homepage, services, contact section, call/WhatsApp buttons, and mobile-friendly design.

I can first send a quick example and then give you a clear price based on what you need.

## “Can you guarantee more customers?”

Reply:

I cannot honestly guarantee customers. What I can do is improve how professional and clear your business looks online, and make it easier for people to contact you. That usually helps, but I prefer to be honest rather than promise numbers.

## “Send me information.”

Reply:

Of course. What email address should I send it to?

I’ll keep it short and include a quick idea for {{business_name}}.

## “Who are you?”

Reply:

My name is {{your_name}}. I work on websites and online presence for local service businesses. I’m reaching out because I noticed {{business_name}} could be a good fit for a cleaner website / better online presentation.

## “We use Facebook / Instagram only.”

Reply:

That can work, especially for existing customers. The reason I suggest a website is that new customers often want one clear place to see services, photos, contact options, and trust signals. A simple website can work together with your Facebook or Instagram.

## “We get enough work already.”

Reply:

That is good to hear. In that case, the website is less about getting more random leads and more about looking professional, filtering better customers, and making it easier for serious customers to contact you.

# 8. Lead Handling Workflow

For each city:

1. Open the workbook.
2. Start with **Send Now**.
3. Then check **No Website Offer**.
4. Then check **Platform Website Offer**.
5. Then check **Manual Review**.
6. Then check **Visual Review**.
7. Ignore **Hard Skip** and **Data Quality Review** unless you are debugging.
8. For each contacted lead, update your manual tracking field if your workflow supports it:
   - not_contacted
   - contacted
   - replied
   - interested
   - not_interested
   - follow_up
   - closed

# 9. Recommended Daily Outreach Routine

## Small daily routine

- 10 businesses reviewed manually
- 5 emails sent
- 2 calls
- 1 follow-up batch

## Better daily routine

- 25 businesses reviewed manually
- 15 emails sent
- 5 calls
- Follow-ups every 3–5 days

## Rule

Quality is more important than volume.

A personalized message to 10 good businesses is better than 100 generic emails.

# 12. Important Notes

- Do not send messages to questionable leads.
- Do not contact businesses from Data Quality Review.
- Do not contact Hard Skip unless you manually confirm it is actually a good business.
- Do not overpromise SEO or lead generation.
- Always be honest.
- Keep emails short.
- Try to move interested people to a call.
- The first goal is a reply, not a sale.
